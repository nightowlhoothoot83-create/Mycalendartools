Frequently Asked Questions

Chinese New Year (Lunar New Year) falls on a different date each year because it follows the lunisolar calendar. It lands between January 21 and February 20. In 2026 it falls on February 17.

2026 is the Year of the Horse. Each of the 12 zodiac animals cycles over a 12-year period.

Celebrations typically last 15 days, ending with the Lantern Festival on the 15th day of the first lunar month.
